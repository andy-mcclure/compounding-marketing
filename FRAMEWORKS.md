# Proprietary Frameworks

## The In-House Growth Machine (IHGM)

The five-part architecture that installs Compounding Marketing inside an independent service business.

### The Five Parts

**1. Command Center**
Strategic foundation and control hub. Contains two components:
- *Strategic Core* — ICA, positioning, offers, competitive context, brand standards
- *Command Core* — AI workspaces, governance rules, access permissions, continuity protocols

**2. Insight**
Analysis engine. Gathers performance data, identifies patterns, surfaces what's working and what isn't. The input layer for the Growth Cycle.

**3. Planning**
Translation layer. Converts insight into prioritized action across annual, quarterly, monthly, and weekly horizons. The 30/90 Planning Rhythm operates here.

**4. Execution**
Execution arm. Content creation, campaign builds, distribution — using AI-assisted workflows inside the owner's environment.

**5. The Growth Cycle**
The repeating operational loop that connects all five parts. Insight informs Planning. Planning directs Execution. Execution generates new data that flows back into Insight. Every cycle makes the next one smarter.

## The 30/90 Planning Rhythm

The operational heartbeat of the IHGM.

- **90-day cycles** set strategic direction — where the business is going and what marketing must accomplish to get there
- **30-day cycles** translate direction into executable actions and adjust based on real performance

The two rhythms interlock: stability at the 90-day level, speed and responsiveness at the 30-day level.

Operational standard: *How is it going? What's next? Make it so.*

## The Marketing Ownership Audit

A proprietary diagnostic that reveals where a business's marketing intelligence currently lives and who controls it.

**What it produces:** A scored assessment identifying gaps between current state and what is possible under a Compounding Marketing model.

**How it works:** Reviews accounts, platforms, AI tool usage, vendor relationships, data ownership, and content workflows to determine the degree of ownership the business actually has.

**Standalone deliverable** — owned by the client regardless of what happens next. A roadmap for closing the gaps is a separate paid engagement.

**Starting point** for any Longview Marketing engagement.

## The Winter Readiness Assessment

Painting contractor variant of the Marketing Ownership Audit.

**The specific problem it addresses:** Painting contractors in cold-climate markets face a predictable annual crisis — exterior work stops, revenue drops, and good crew members leave for steadier work. The businesses that survive winter without losing crew are the ones with a system for generating interior work before exterior season ends.

**What it surfaces:** Gaps in the marketing system relative to what it would take to fill a winter calendar with interior painting work — before winter arrives.

**Urgency driver:** Calendar. The window to build the pipeline closes when exterior season ends.

**Diagnosis only.** Action plan is a separate paid engagement.

## The Living Intelligence Layer

The core intellectual foundation underneath all frameworks.

For 17 years, Andy McClure operated as a human Living Intelligence Layer for his clients — a continuously adapting, business-aware intelligence sitting between generic marketing services and the specific, nuanced reality of each company. Built through deep listening. Maintained through a repeatable rhythm. Alive because the business is alive.

The insight that produced the IHGM: AI is how the Living Intelligence Layer finally becomes scalable. Not by replacing the intelligence — by making it possible for the intelligence to live inside the client's environment instead of inside one person's head.

## The Interview-First Method

The signature entry point and the origin of the Living Intelligence Layer for each client.

Before strategy, before planning, before any content — interview the client's clients. Get the vocabulary directly from the people who chose this business. Understand what mattered to them, why they chose this provider over others, what they valued most.

This is where accurate positioning begins. The language that resonates isn't invented by the marketer — it's spoken by the customer. The Interview-First Method captures it at the source.

## The Growth Cycle

The repeating operational loop at the center of the IHGM.

1. Review business status and marketing performance
2. Merge into a plan
3. Execute the activities in that plan
4. Feed results back into the next review

Month after month, the system grows more accurate, more sensitive, more specific to the business it serves. The slope forms because the cycle never breaks.

## The Agency Trap

The structural condition that creates reset.

Most agencies build capability on their side that the client will never own. The client rents access to tools, systems, and intelligence that accumulate somewhere else. When they leave, they start from zero.

The trap is not the agency. The trap is the structure — and most clients don't recognize it until they've reset two or three times.

---

## The Luxury Trap

The reason the Living Intelligence Layer is hard to keep.

The layer is invisible when it's working, which makes it look cuttable. It sits on top of services clients could theoretically buy directly, so it reads as overhead. Until it's gone — and then the marketing slowly stops reflecting the business. The degradation is gradual enough that clients rarely connect the cause to the effect.

---

## The Frankenstein Situation

What happens when the pure advisor model is taken too far.

The pure advisor creates a monster: the client becomes the hub of a three-to-five vendor system they don't have the expertise to manage. The advisor orchestrates from outside. Nobody owns the coherence. The Living Intelligence Layer needs to be inside the machine, not observing from a parallel universe.

Absorbing the complexity is the act of service — not standing apart from it in the name of purity.
