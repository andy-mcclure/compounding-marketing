# Voice Standards

---

## The Voice

Andy McClure's voice is the voice of an engineer who learned to communicate.

Precise without being cold. Structured without being rigid. He explains things the way someone explains a system — clearly, sequentially, with a point of view about what matters and what doesn't.

He is not a hype marketer. He does not inflate claims or manufacture urgency. He has a perspective. He shares it plainly. He does not hedge when something is clear.

Short sentences when making a point. Longer sentences when building an argument. No rhetorical questions as a substitute for a position. One analogy per idea, used precisely. Acknowledges complexity without hiding behind it.

- *"The intelligence walks out the door with them."*
- *"Marketing isn't broken. It's just that nobody owns it."*
- *"You don't need to become a marketer. You need to own the system where marketing happens."*
- *"The architect doesn't pour concrete. The architect ensures the concrete gets poured in the right place at the right time for the right reason."*
- *"AI doesn't create strategy. It accelerates execution inside a structure that already makes sense."*
- *"Two questions and one sentence: How is it going? What's next? Make it so."*
- *"Tactics give you a spike that fades. Systems give you a slope that compounds."*
- *"You're not building an asset. You're renting someone else's."*
- *"Same investment. Completely different outcome."*
- *"The race isn't who adopted AI first. The race is who built a system that compounds AI benefits over time."*
- *"I have engineered myself into a role as one of the cogs in the machine."*
- *"The methodology wasn't designed. It was discovered."*
- *"Hard to read the label from inside the jar."*
- *"Every transaction is really a transformation at some level."*
- *"The layer that I provided seemed like a luxury."*

## Language Andy Does Not Use

- "Game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "disruptive"
- "Crush it" / "dominate" / "kill it"
- "Plug and play"
- "Set it and forget it"
- "Full-service agency"
- "Best practices"
- "Synergy" / "leverage" (as verb) / "ecosystem"
- "VP of Marketing" as the owner's role

---

## Tone Markers

- Short sentences when making a point
- Longer sentences when building an argument
- No rhetorical questions as a substitute for a position
- One analogy per idea, used precisely
- Acknowledges complexity without hiding behind it
- States conclusions first, then supports them
- Does not soften claims that are accurate
- Does not inflate claims that require evidence
